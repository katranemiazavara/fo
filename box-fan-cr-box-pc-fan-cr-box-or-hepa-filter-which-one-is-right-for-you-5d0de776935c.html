For portable air filtration devices, we have a few options at our disposal and some work better than others in different situations. Here’s a breakdown of when each one can work best.

Box Fan Corsi-Rosenthal Box

This is the original CR box that has been assembled for schools and homes everywhere. It is the cheapest method we have to clean the air and is extremely powerful — it beats out pretty much every HEPA filter that it is tested against. Anyone can build it and it is extremely important from an equity perspective — it makes clean air available to everyone. However, it does not have a low noise level at 47 dB (although much lower than most HEPA filters on high). You can address this with a fan like the Honeywell HF-910, but those fans are more expensive and difficult to assemble, which negates the main advantages of the CR box.

Advantages:

  • low cost (<$100)
  • extremely high clean air delivery rate (>400 cubic feet per minute [CFM])
  • easy to assemble and scale up
  • filters can be purchased anywhere
  • long filter life (6 months)
  • good throw (air speed) leading to better air distribution

Disadvantages:

  • take up lots of space (20" x 20")
  • minimum noise is >47 dB. Tolerable in some settings, but not all
  • aesthetic issues for some

Best places to use:

  • large spaces
  • spaces with lots of background noise
  • classrooms (where noise is tolerable)

PC Fan Corsi-Rosenthal Box

These are a relatively new invention. If you want to buy one, you can get it at https://www.cleanairkits.com/ . People have been DIYing them, but it isn’t as easy.

What you ultimately care when selecting portable air cleaners are: clean air delivery rate, noise and cost. PC fans are extremely quiet and this is the best way to get a high clean air delivery rate with a very little noise. When it comes to selecting the ideal portable air cleaner, PC fan CR boxes are the best option.

Advantages:

  • very quiet (40–43 dBA)
  • high clean air delivery rate (200–480 CFM)
  • does not take up much space
  • filters can be purchased anywhere

Disadvantages:

  • much higher cost than box-fan CR box, but better value than commercial HEPA filters
  • difficult to scale up and build
  • not very sturdy
  • shorter filter life

Best places to use:

  • residential settings
  • all other settings when it becomes more scalable

Commercial HEPA Filters

These are the most common and you can find everywhere. Not all HEPA filters are created equal. Some are really good and some are overpriced noisemakers. For most of them, they give a descent clean air delivery rate with really high noise and a poor clean air delivery rate on low speeds with low noise. People generally turn them down to low speeds and they are not that effective. Many come with unnecessary or even potentially add-ons. For a given clean air delivery rate, they are generally much more expensive than different types of CR boxes. Many organizations have unjustifiably shown apprehension to the use of CR Boxes, so these are an alternative when CR boxes are not an option. Here’s a comparison of many of the more common ones.

Being able to provide a good clean air delivery rate while ensuring noise is acceptable is difficult but it can be done. It generally requires purchasing many of them and running them on low, which will increase cost. If you want to design a space with portable HEPA filters, go to https://cleanairstars.com/filters/

Advantages:

  • no assembly required
  • easy to purchase en masse
  • sturdy

Disadvantages:

  • much higher cost than CR boxes and lower flow
  • higher cost for filter replacement
  • generally poor noise profile for reasonable clean air delivery rates. This can be solved.
  • often include unnecessary or bad features

Best places to use:

  • can be sized for any space, but expensive if used for large spaces.

Summary

This isn’t a one-size fits all solution. The most effective and cost effective unit is the original box fan CR box. But it requires assembly and its noise level is not appropriate for all settings. In my opinion, we need a better box fan — a built-in shroud and noise of 43 dB or less, even if it needs to be run slower.

The best units for residential uses are the PC fan CR boxes, but they are currently harder to scale and are more expensive. If you care about how achieving a high clean air delivery rate with low noise and taking up little space, these are the best option.

Portable commercial HEPA filters can be used anywhere, you just need to account for noise considerations while purchasing them. This will likely mean purchasing multiple ones for a given space and running them on low to achieve effective clean air delivery rates without high levels of noise.
